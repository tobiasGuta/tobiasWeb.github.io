What is the definition of hacking?

A few years ago, the term 'hacking' was often misunderstood as something unethical. It is most commonly associated with illegal activities and data theft perpetrated by cybercriminals.

In cybersecurity, hacking refers to the misuse of devices such as computers, Internet of Things (IoT) devices, and networks to steal data, documents, or disrupt data activities.

Different Types of Hackers

In the hacking community, hackers are generally categorized into three main types:

  • White Hat Hackers: These ethical hackers use their skills to help organizations secure their systems.
  • Grey Hat Hackers: These hackers fall somewhere in between; they may exploit vulnerabilities without permission but often report them afterward.
  • Black Hat Hackers: These individuals engage in malicious activities, exploiting vulnerabilities for personal gain.
